Traditional Mochi

Time-honored recipes rooted in Japanese and Okinawan heritage. Our traditional line traces its lineage directly to a Buddhist priest trained in Japan — passed down to Aunty Tomi, then Aunty Nora, and now Emerald and Clyde. Classics like Daifuku, Nantu, and Yomogi are made exactly as they always have been: by hand, with care, and without shortcuts.

Manju (Baked Confection)

Traditional Japanese baked sweets with tender golden crusts and sweet bean fillings. Unlike mochi, manju are baked rather than steamed or pounded — resulting in a soft pastry exterior that contrasts beautifully with the rich an filling inside. Our Taiyaki, shaped like the iconic Japanese fish, is a weekend-only special that draws lines on its own.

Fruit Mochi

Whole fresh fruit — a real, uncut strawberry — wrapped in hand-mashed azuki paste and pillowy mochi dough. This is the item Two Ladies Kitchen is most famous for, featured in Food Network, Honolulu Magazine, and countless visitor videos. Seasonal fruits rotate throughout the year. Please pre-order — fruit mochi sells out early and must be enjoyed the same day.
